Mahatma Gandhi

lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869- Birth at Porbandar.
  • 1888- Reached Southampton, England.
  • 1893- Thrown off the first class carriage of the train at Pietermartizburg Station.
  • 1894- Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'.
  • 1896- Public lecture at Poona; chaired by Ramkrishna Bhandarkar.
  • 1906- Advent of Satyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, where people took a pledge with God as their witness, to oppose the Black Act.
  • 1913- MKG released from jail unconditionally with others, before the completion of the period of imprisonement.
  • 1918- Appointed to arbitar the dispute between Mill-owners and Mill-hands of Ahmedabad.
  • 1922- Bardoli Taluka resolved against payment of land revenue and Civil-Disobedience.
  • 1925- The publication of 'Autobiography or The Story of My Experiments with Truth' commences in Young India.
  • 1931- Attended (in his loin-cloth) the Royal reception for the delegates of the Second Round Table Conference at the Buckingham Palace at the invitation of Emperor George V.
  • 1939- Sir Maurice Gwyer declared award in favour of the people of Rajkot; Muslims and Bhayats demonstrate against Gandhiji and disrupt a prayer meeting.
  • 1941- The Congress Working Committee at Bardoli accepted Gandhiji's request to free him from the responsibility of guiding the Congress.
  • 1946- Handed over the responsibility of Harijan Journals to Kaka Kalelkar, Kishorelal Mashruwala and Narahari Parikh.
  • 1947- The Congress, the Muslim League and the representatives of the Sikhs accept the plan of Partition.
  • 1948- Cremated on the banks of the Yamuna by Ramdas.
